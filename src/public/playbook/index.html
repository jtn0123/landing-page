PASS 1. Spread Flood Right
Formation: Spread (2x2 + RB)
Classic flood concept — three receivers attack three levels on the right side (flat, intermediate, deep). Forces the defense to cover high-low and puts a single defender in a bind.
Read Progression
① WR4 flat route — quick check if defender bails deep
② WR3 corner route — main money throw vs zone
③ RB wheel — the home run if defense bites short
Best Against
Cover 2 and Cover 3 zone. The flat/corner combo is nearly impossible to cover with one defender.
When to Call
2nd & medium, or anytime you see zone coverage. Great near the red zone.
📱 Playmaker X Guide

Formation Setup

  • WR1: far left, on the line of scrimmage
  • WR2: left slot, 1 yard off the line
  • WR3: right slot, 1 yard off the line
  • WR4: far right, on the line
  • RB: 3 yards behind QB, offset right

Route Drawing

  • WR1: draw a go route straight up the left sideline (~20 yds) — clearing route
  • WR2: draw a short in route at 6 yards — clearing route
  • WR3: draw up 10 yards, then angle right toward the sideline at 45° for 8 yards (corner route)
  • WR4: draw straight out to the right sideline at 3 yards (flat route)
  • RB: draw a wheel route — release right, curve upfield along the right sideline for 15+ yards

Read Progression

  • 1st read: WR4 flat
  • 2nd read: WR3 corner
  • 3rd read: RB wheel

PASS 2. Trips Right — Levels
Formation: Trips Right (3x1 + RB)
Three receivers at three different depths on the same side — short out, medium dig, deep out. The QB reads the underneath defenders and throws to the open level.
Read Progression
① WR2 short out — quick and easy if flat is open
② WR3 dig/in at ~10 yards — vs soft middle zone
③ WR4 deep out at ~15 yards — if defense sits on short stuff
Best Against
Any zone coverage. The stacked levels make it nearly impossible to cover all three with two defenders.
When to Call
3rd & medium (5-8 yards). Safe, high-percentage concept.

PASS 3. Bunch Right — Mesh
Formation: Bunch Right (3 close + WR1 isolated left)
Two receivers cross underneath each other (mesh/pick concept). The bunch formation creates natural rubs that free up receivers against man coverage. In flag football, picks are legal through bunching.
Read Progression
① WR2 shallow cross right-to-left — usually open first
② WR1 shallow cross left-to-right — the crosser going opposite
③ WR3 wheel up — if defense collapses on the mesh
Best Against
Man coverage. The crossing routes create traffic that man defenders can't navigate cleanly.
When to Call
Any down. Your bread-and-butter man-beater. Great in short yardage and red zone.

  • WR3: bunch right — middle, 1 yard off the line, 1 yard outside WR2

  • WR3: draw a wheel route — release outside, curve upfield along the right sideline for 15 yards

  • RB: draw a flat route to the left at 3 yards — checkdown

PASS 4. Spread — Four Verticals
Formation: Spread (2x2 + RB deep)
All four WRs run vertical routes, stretching the defense deep. The two inside seam routes are the primary reads — QB reads the safeties and throws to whichever seam is uncovered.
Read Progression
① WR2 left seam — read the middle safety; throw opposite of where he leans
② WR3 right seam — the other seam option
③ WR1 outside go — if the corner plays soft
Best Against
Cover 3 (the seams split the two deep zones). Also good vs single-high looks.
When to Call
When you need a big play. 1st down, or when the defense is playing tight/short. Requires a QB who can throw deep.

PASS 5. Trips Left — Smash
Formation: Trips Left (3x1 + RB)
Classic smash concept: a hitch/corner combination. WR1 runs a short hitch to hold the corner, and WR2 runs a corner route behind him. If the corner sits on the hitch, throw the corner. If he bails, take the hitch.
Read Progression
① WR1 hitch at 5 yards — quick throw if corner plays deep
② WR2 corner route — the money throw if corner bites on the hitch
③ WR3 slant inside — safety valve middle
Best Against
Cover 2 (the corner route splits the corner and safety). Also effective vs Cover 4.
When to Call
3rd & short to medium. Nearly uncoverable if the QB makes the right read.

PASS 6. Stack Left — Quick Slants
Formation: Stack Left (WR1 over WR2) + 2 right
Quick-hitting slants from both sides. The stack formation on the left creates a natural pick — WR1 runs the slant while WR2 peels out underneath. Fast throw, low risk, gains 5-8 yards consistently.
Read Progression
① WR1 quick slant — throw on the break, very fast
② WR3 slant from right — the other option if left is covered
③ WR2 out from stack — sneaks out underneath for the dump-off
Best Against
Man coverage and soft zones. Quick timing beats the rush.
When to Call
3rd & short. Also great as an opening play to establish quick rhythm. One of the safest plays in the book.

PASS 7. Spread — Post/Dig Combo
Formation: Spread (2x2 + RB deep)
The post/dig combination attacks the middle of the field at two depths. WR3 runs a deep post to pull the safety, and WR2 runs a dig underneath into the vacated space. Classic high-low on the middle defender.
Read Progression
① WR3 post route — the big play if the safety doesn't get over
② WR2 dig at ~12 yards — reliable throw into the void left by the safety
③ WR1 out — boundary safety valve
Best Against
Cover 1 / Cover 3. The post takes the deep middle, the dig sits underneath.
When to Call
Any down, especially when you want to attack the middle of the field. Great on 2nd & long.

RUN 8. Spread — QB Draw
Formation: Spread (2x2 + RB)
All receivers sell go routes to clear defenders deep. QB takes the snap, pauses a beat like it's a pass play, then tucks and runs straight up the middle. Works best when the defense is expecting pass.
Key Points
• WRs MUST sell the go route — sprint hard to pull DBs deep
• QB: 1-2 count after snap, then go
• RB provides a lead block or takes a fake handoff
Best Against
Teams that rush aggressively or drop into deep coverage. The lanes open up when everyone clears out.
When to Call
2nd & medium, or after hitting several passes in a row. Change of pace. Works great with a mobile QB.

RUN 9. Trips Right — Jet Sweep
Formation: Trips Right + WR1 motions from left
WR1 motions across the formation pre-snap. On the snap, QB hands off (or pitches) to WR1 who sweeps to the right behind blockers. Put your fastest player at WR1.
Key Points
• WR1 must time the motion — at full speed at the snap
• WR3 and WR4 block the edge
• RB fakes a run the opposite direction
• WR2 clears out a go route to take a defender deep
Best Against
Slow defenses or teams that over-commit to the pass. Gets the ball to your athlete in space.
When to Call
1st down. Change of pace. Especially effective after establishing a passing attack. Get your speedster the ball.

RUN 10. Spread — Run/Pass Option
Formation: Spread (2x2 + RB)
QB reads the edge defender. If the defender crashes inside or sits, QB scrambles right for easy yards. If the defender comes up to stop the run, QB throws the quick out to WR4 or the slant to WR3.
Key Points
• Read the right-side defender at the snap
• ① WR4 quick out — throw if defender crashes on QB
• ② WR3 slant — alternative pass option
• If both defenders bail → just run it
Best Against
Aggressive defenses. Puts one defender in an unwinnable position.
When to Call
Short yardage, 3rd & 3-5. Also great near the goal line. Requires an athletic QB.

TRICK 11. Trips Left — Double Pass
Formation: Trips Left + WR4 isolated right
QB throws a quick screen left to WR1. WR2 and WR3 sell the screen by blocking. WR1 catches it, stops, and throws deep downfield to WR4 who has beaten his man on a go route. The defense bites on the screen and WR4 is wide open.
Key Points
• WR1 MUST be able to throw — practice this!
• WR4 sells a lazy start then explodes deep
• Timing: WR1 needs 2 seconds to catch, set, and throw
• Only works once per game — save it for a big moment
Best Against
Aggressive defenses that rally to the screen. Man coverage where the corner on WR4 cheats toward the screen.
When to Call
Need a big play. Down 7 in the 4th quarter. Once per game MAX. ⚠️ Check league rules — some don't allow forward passes after a lateral.

Formation Setup

  • WR1: far left (trips left — outside), on the line
  • WR2: left slot (trips left — middle), 1 yard off the line
  • WR3: left slot (trips left — inside), 1 yard off the line
  • WR4: far right, on the line (isolated)
  • RB: 3 yards behind QB

Route Drawing

  • WR1: draw a short screen route — 2 yards behind the line toward the left sideline, then add a second throw arrow from WR1 deep downfield to WR4 (use trick/purple color)
  • WR2: draw a block symbol near WR1 (screen blocking)
  • WR3: draw a block symbol near WR1 (screen blocking)
  • WR4: draw a go route straight up the right sideline 25+ yards
  • RB: draw a flat release right — checkdown/decoy

Read Progression

  • QB throws screen to WR1 (1st action)
  • WR1's read: WR4 deep go (only throw)

TRICK 12. Spread — Fake Screen & Go
Formation: Spread (2x2 + RB)
Set up the screen look to the right — WR3 and WR4 fake a screen (WR3 moves to "block"). QB pump fakes the screen. WR4 plants and takes off deep. Defense bites on the screen and WR4 is gone.
Key Points
• QB pump fake must be convincing
• WR4 takes 2 steps toward sideline (sell the screen), then go
• WR3 fakes the block then slips upfield as a secondary option
• Set this up by running actual screens earlier in the game
Best Against
Teams that aggressively jump screens. If they bit on a real screen earlier, this will burn them.
When to Call
After running 2-3 actual screens to the right side. The more they've seen the screen, the harder they'll bite.

TRICK 13. Spread — Hook & Lateral
Formation: Spread (2x2 + RB)
WR3 runs a hook route at ~10 yards and catches the ball. As defenders converge, WR4 is trailing behind on a designed route. WR3 laterals to WR4 who has a clear lane upfield. Looks like a normal hook play until the lateral.
Key Points
• WR4 must trail at the right depth — not too close, not too far
• WR3 catches, makes one fake upfield, then pitches
• The lateral must go backward — practice the toss!
• WR4 should be your fastest player
Best Against
Man coverage where defenders turn their back to the ball after the catch. Zone teams that relax after the completion.
When to Call
Desperation or fun moment. Late in the game when you need a spark. Risky — the lateral can go wrong. Practice it.
